2024.09: Completed Bachelor of Science in Statistics at The University of British Columbia. Currently preparing for PhD applications to advance research in industrial monitoring and social system analysis.

About Me

I am a recent graduate in Statistics with a deep interest in data science, machine learning, and causal inference. Through hands-on research in both industrial and social data contexts, I have developed a versatile skill set in data-driven modeling, complex systems analysis, and scientific decision-making.

Under the mentorship of Dr. Liang Cao, I conducted research on interpretable soft sensors, applying models like SHAP and Extremely Randomized Trees to enhance transparency in industrial processes. This research was presented at conferences such as IFAC and CCEC and has fostered my passion for translating complex data into actionable insights. My research has also extended to social systems, where I worked with Professor Mia Raynard on analyzing global media sentiment in relation to regulatory policies, employing statistical techniques like MANOVA and causal inference tools such as gCastle.

Driven by the goal of using data to unravel complex phenomena, my work bridges the realms of industrial process optimization and social sentiment analysis, contributing to more informed, data-backed decision-making.

On-Going Projects

Adaptive Process Monitoring for Multiphase Industrial Processes

In collaboration with Dr. Liang Cao, this project focuses on developing adaptive soft sensor models that integrate Gaussian Mixture Models and Change Point Detection for real-time monitoring in multi-stage industrial environments. These models provide accurate predictions across varying operational conditions, improving both reliability and operational efficiency.

Media Sentiment Analysis and Causal Inference in Regulatory Events

Working with Professor Mia Raynard, I am exploring the impact of regulatory events on public sentiment towards alternative proteins. Using causal inference and survival analysis, we are quantifying the patterns of sentiment change post-regulation, providing a nuanced view of media influence on public opinion.
